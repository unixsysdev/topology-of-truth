\title{From ``Truth Topology'' to Readout Bottlenecks:\\
Negative Replication on GSM8K and a Positive Representation--Decoder Dissociation in Procedural Micro-Worlds}
\author{Anonymous}
\date{April 2026}

\begin{document}
\maketitle
\onehalfspacing

\begin{abstract}
We report a three-phase investigation of hidden-state geometry for reasoning correctness and semantic truth status.
Phase A tested a global ``topology of truth'' hypothesis on GSM8K-style traces; this branch failed under stronger controls.
Dynamic and fixed-window $H_0$ summaries were weak or degenerate and did not outperform simple controls after confound checks.
Phase B, under fixed decoding, showed a different strong effect: token-cap termination was the dominant operational predictor of error, indicating non-convergence as the primary failure mode.
Phase C introduced a procedural micro-world benchmark with nonce lexicons, held-out templates, and exact \texttt{True}/\texttt{False}/\texttt{Unknown} labels.
Across Qwen and Gemma families, decoder behavior systematically under-expressed \texttt{Unknown}, while verdict-region hidden-state probes recovered substantial \texttt{Unknown} signal on held-out worlds.
This dissociation survived prompt-path controls, constrained decoding, base-vs-instruct checks, verdict-step label-logit analysis, and layer sweeps.
The positive contribution is therefore not a universal scalar ``truth topology,'' but a replicated representation--decoder mismatch for non-entailment.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
This repository started from a broad claim: correct reasoning might have a globally coherent topological signature in hidden-state trajectories.
That claim did not survive replication-quality checks.
The final evidence supports a narrower and stronger statement:
\begin{quote}
On a controlled semantic task, non-entailment (\texttt{Unknown}) is encoded in internal representations but systematically under-expressed by decoder outputs.
\end{quote}

We report the full trajectory rather than hiding failures:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Phase A (negative):} global trace topology did not yield a reliable correctness signal.
  \item \textbf{Phase B (positive):} fixed-decoding non-convergence strongly predicted wrong answers.
  \item \textbf{Phase C (main positive):} replicated representation--decoder dissociation for \texttt{Unknown} in a held-out procedural semantic benchmark.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Research Questions}
\textbf{RQ1 (failed):} Can global topological summaries of natural-language reasoning traces predict correctness robustly across conditions?

\textbf{RQ2 (supported):} In a controlled semantic benchmark, is \texttt{Unknown} / non-entailment represented in hidden states even when decoder behavior collapses that class?

\section{Phase A: Global Topology on GSM8K (Negative Replication)}
\subsection{Setup}
Phase A used paired Qwen3.5-0.8B/2B pilot traces and computed static/dynamic topological summaries from hidden-state clouds.
The main files are in:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{artifacts/paired\_dynamic\_qwen35/}
  \item \texttt{artifacts/qwen35\_2b\_window\_corrected/}
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Result}
On non-capped 2B pilot traces, dynamic metrics were weak:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{h0\_entropy\_final AUC=0.55}
  \item \texttt{peak\_h0\_entropy AUC=0.55}
  \item \texttt{auc\_h0\_entropy\_per\_token AUC=0.50}
  \item \texttt{max\_delta\_h0 AUC=0.60}
\end{itemize}

After fixing window preprocessing, topology remained non-competitive:
\begin{itemize}
  \item controls-only AUC: 0.60
  \item corrected-topology-only AUC: 0.20
  \item controls+corrected-topology AUC: 0.20
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Interpretation}
The global hypothesis failed because the measured summaries were dominated by construction and length effects, not stable correctness-related geometry.
This branch is retained as a negative replication record.

\section{Phase B: Fixed-Decoding Convergence Branch}
\subsection{Setup}
We ran Qwen3.5-2B with fixed decoding:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{temperature=0.7}
  \item \texttt{top\_p=0.95}
  \item \texttt{max\_new\_tokens=384}
  \item 20 questions $\times$ 9 samples/question ($n=180$ total)
\end{itemize}

Primary manifest:
\texttt{artifacts/qwen35\_2b\_within\_question/run\_manifest.csv}

\subsection{Outcome and equation}
Define
\[
C = \mathbb{1}[\text{termination is cap}], \qquad Y = \mathbb{1}[\text{wrong answer}]
\]
Counts:
\begin{itemize}
  \item capped: 87 (75 wrong / 12 correct)
  \item EOS: 93 (17 wrong / 76 correct)
\end{itemize}
The operational signal is large:
\[
\mathrm{AUC}(C \rightarrow Y)=0.839
\]
and the odds ratio is
\[
\mathrm{OR}
=
\frac{\frac{75}{12}}{\frac{17}{76}}
\approx 26.40.
\]

\subsection{Cap sensitivity at longer budget}
We reran all originally capped samples at \texttt{max\_new\_tokens=640} with matched IDs/seeds:
\begin{itemize}
  \item rerun set: \(n=87\), in \texttt{artifacts/qwen35\_2b\_cap\_sensitivity\_640/}
  \item among originally capped wrong (75): 27 flipped wrong$\rightarrow$correct, 48 stayed wrong
  \item still capped at 640 remained heavily wrong (24 wrong / 4 correct)
\end{itemize}
Conclusion: truncation matters, but deeper non-convergence remains a dominant regime.

\section{Phase C: Procedural Micro-World Benchmark}
\subsection{Dataset design}
To isolate semantics from benchmark contamination, we procedurally generate latent worlds and render language statements over nonce symbols.
The dataset generator is:
\texttt{scripts/generate\_micro\_world\_dataset.py}
with outputs under
\texttt{artifacts/micro\_world\_v1/dataset/}.

Each world has:
\begin{itemize}
  \item entities and attributes with split-specific nonce lexicon,
  \item binary relations with held-out lexical variants,
  \item 9 propositions and 8 paraphrases/proposition,
  \item exact labels \(y \in \{\texttt{True},\texttt{False},\texttt{Unknown}\}\).
\end{itemize}
Thus each world contributes 72 examples.

Main sweeps use 20 train worlds and 20 eval worlds (1,440 eval examples/model).

\section{Methods}
\subsection{Stored inference artifacts}
Per example we store prompt text, gold label, decoder label, parse status, verdict-region hidden states, and verdict-step logits.
The canonical per-sample files are:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \texttt{sample.json}
  \item \texttt{verdict\_states.npz}
  \item \texttt{logits\_summary.npz}
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Linear probe}
For one state key, with hidden vector \(h_i \in \mathbb{R}^d\) and class \(y_i \in \{1,2,3\}\), the probe is:
\[
s_i = Wh_i + b,\quad W\in\mathbb{R}^{3\times d},\ b\in\mathbb{R}^{3},
\]
\[
\hat y_i = \arg\max_c (s_i)_c.
\]
Training minimizes multiclass cross-entropy:
\[
\mathcal{L}(W,b) = -\sum_i
\log \frac{\exp((Wh_i+b)_{y_i})}{\sum_c \exp((Wh_i+b)_c)}.
\]
This asks whether class information is linearly recoverable from hidden states, without modifying the base model.

\subsection{Within-world geometry gap}
Within each world and state key, we compute:
\[
D_{\text{same}} = \mathbb{E}[d(h_i,h_j)\mid y_i=y_j], \quad
D_{\text{diff}} = \mathbb{E}[d(h_i,h_j)\mid y_i\neq y_j],
\]
\[
\Delta = D_{\text{diff}} - D_{\text{same}}.
\]
\(\Delta>0\) implies tighter same-label neighborhoods than different-label neighborhoods.
We report sign-test consistency across worlds.

\subsection{Verdict-step label-logit metrics}
For label logits \((\ell_T,\ell_F,\ell_U)\):
\[
P_U = \frac{e^{\ell_U}}{e^{\ell_T}+e^{\ell_F}+e^{\ell_U}},
\qquad
m_U = \ell_U - \max(\ell_T,\ell_F).
\]
Negative \(m_U\) means \texttt{Unknown} is under-ranked at the final decision step.

\subsection{Layer sweep}
For each transformer layer \(\ell\), we extract the same state family and evaluate the same linear probe protocol:
\[
R_U^{(\ell)} = \text{probe recall for Unknown at layer }\ell.
\]
This identifies where non-entailment is maximally linearly recoverable.

\section{Interpretation}
The evidence supports a representation--readout bottleneck:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item non-entailment is encoded internally,
  \item the decoder under-ranks/under-emits that class,
  \item this mismatch replicates across model families and controls.
\end{enumerate}

What is \textbf{not} supported:
\begin{itemize}
  \item a universal scalar ``topology of truth,''
  \item a claim that topology alone is the dominant predictive signal on GSM8K traces.
\end{itemize}

\section{Limitations}
\begin{itemize}
  \item Model coverage is small-to-mid scale only.
  \item The procedural benchmark is controlled but synthetic.
  \item Probe recoverability does not alone prove causal use by decoder policy.
\end{itemize}

\section{Conclusion}
The original global-topology headline did not survive.
The validated result is a replicated representation--decoder dissociation:
on a held-out procedural semantic benchmark, \texttt{Unknown} is strongly recoverable from hidden states while decoder outputs systematically under-express it.
This reframes the work from ``truth topology'' to readout bottlenecks in semantic uncertainty expression.
